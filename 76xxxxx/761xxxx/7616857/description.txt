Maria Luisa Bemberg is most noted for her study of Sor Juana Ines de la Cruz (I, the Worst of All), but she also made a number of movies set in her native Argentina. This one is unusual for being mostly in English.

Julie Christie plays an English governess who escapes depression-era England by taking a job with a family of rich cattle-barons on the Argentine pampas. She is quite unused to the feudal conditions and the insufferable class snobbery. Part of this is the patriarch's decision that the family will live its life in English, thus suiting it to the modern world.

But great changes are in store for the country, and the family...
