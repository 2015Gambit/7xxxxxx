Mrs Biggs is a 2012 television series written by Jeff Pope based on the true story
of the woman behind Britains most famous villain, Great Train Robber Ronnie Biggs
and journey from naïve young woman at the hands of an overbearing father to the joy
of freedom and motherhood as a gangsters moll.

Ronnie returns from his tree felling job and Charmian realises he has been involved
in the Great Train Robbery confirmed when he shows her his share, £147,000. Charmian
lives in fear of discovery and they disperse the money amongst trusted friend.
September 1963 and Ronnie is arrested and Charmian is beseiged by the press and she
is drawn into Bruce Reynolds, who has disappeared, web of friends. They deal with a
chancer demanding money from her for protection. At the trial Ronnie is given 30 years
imprisonment and Charmian is disowned by her father and mother.
