"The Ecstasy of Gold" (Italian: L'Estasi dell'Oro) is a musical composition by Ennio Morricone, part of his score for the Sergio Leone film The Good, the Bad and the Ugly. It is played while Tuco (Eli Wallach) is frantically searching a graveyard for the grave that holds $200,000 in gold coins, and also during some of the other scenes in the film. The song is played as Tuco and Blondie (Clint Eastwood) are walking through the desert, and additionally over the closing credits. It stands as one of the most well known of Ennio Morricone's themes, and indeed as one of the most famous musical compositions in the history of cinema.

Remixed by Bandini for the Ennio Morricone Remixes 2 album released on February 10, 2004 by Compost Records. Used in the introduction sequence of the 2010 Activision racing game, Blur.
