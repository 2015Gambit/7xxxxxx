Monty Python was a British surreal comedy group who created Monty Python's
Flying Circus, a British television comedy sketch show that first aired on
the BBC on 5 October 1969.
The television series, broadcast by the BBC from 1969 to 1974, was conceived,
written and performed by members Graham Chapman, John Cleese, Terry Gilliam,
Eric Idle, Terry Jones, and Michael Palin.
