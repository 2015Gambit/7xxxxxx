

Maud is Tennyson's map of a rough, disturbed mid-Victorian consciousness, pointing towards Browning's psychological portraits and twentieth century 'stream of consciousness' techniques.

Here it is read by Joseph Millson in a BBC production from 2009, the bicentennial year of Tennyson's birth.

Go to this link at Wikipedia for a neat and concise summary and interpretation - http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maud_and_other_poems
