Law Enforcement
29.
A body may refuse to indicate whether or not it holds a record, or refuse to communicate
information, where to do so would, or would be likely to, cause serious prejudice to: ΓÇô
(a) the prevention or detection of crime;
(b) the apprehension or prosecution of offenders;
(c) the administration of justice;
(d) the assessment or collection of any tax or duty;
(e) the operation of immigration controls; or
(f) the assessment by a public body of whether civil or criminal proceedings, or
regulatory action pursuant to any enactment, would be justified.
