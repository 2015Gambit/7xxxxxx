Stompin' Grounds is the fourth studio album released by American country 
rock band The Kentucky Headhunters. It was the first album to feature Doug 
Phelps on lead vocals. He had rejoined the band after leaving Brother Phelps, 
a duo founded by him and his brother Ricky Lee, who was originally their lead 
vocalist. The only single from it was a cover of Guy Mitchell's "Singin' the 
Blues".
