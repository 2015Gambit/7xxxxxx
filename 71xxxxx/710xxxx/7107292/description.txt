Live at Leeds is The Who's first live album, and is the only live album that was released 
while the group were still recording and performing regularly. Initially released in the 
United States on 16 May 1970, by Decca and MCA and the United Kingdom on 23 May 1970, by 
Track and Polydor, the album has been reissued on several occasions and in several different 
formats. As of 2005, the album is ranked number 170 on Rolling Stone magazine's list of 
the 500 greatest albums of all time.

It is often cited as the best live rock album of all time and is included in the book 1001 
Albums You Must Hear Before You Die. It is also included in Q magazine's list of Loudest 
Albums of All Time.
