Aimer et Perdre ~ To Love & To Lose, Songs 1917-1934 ~ 2CD (Tompkins Square) [FLAC] {MKOP}

''This is one from the heart. The unique pre-war music of the Cajun bayous, the Carpathian Mountains of Ukraine and Poland, and the American rural countryside has been collected to narrate the human odyssey of love gained and love lost. Early songs of unbridled anticipation and desperate longing color the canvas of love, courtship, dejection and marriage… a never-ending cycle.'' 

Drawn from the Carpathian Mountains, the Cajun bayou and the rural Appalachians, this two-disc set of 78s released between 1917 and 1934 is made coherent by its theme of love found, lost, abandoned, and mourned, and with tracks like the desperately wracked "False Hearted Lover's Blues" by Dock Boggs, Emry Arthur's "She Lied to Me," and the Carter Family's "I Never Will Marry," it makes for a kind of alternative, rural, and often spooky journey through the joyous, sad, and sometimes tragic tangles of love.-Steve Leggett (Allmusic)
